# Software Specification Sheet: Generative Sleep Music Synthesis Engine

---

## 1. System Overview & Technology Stack

* 
**Primary Framework:** `Tone.js` (latest stable release) for sample-accurate scheduling, audio routing, and envelope modulation.


* **Environment:** Web Audio API compliant browser environments.
* 
**Target Architecture:** Single-page web application architecture driven by a centralized `GenerativeAudioController` managing two sub-engines (Pitch and Temporal) coupled to the `Tone.Transport` timeline.



---

## 2. Audio Graph & Voice Register Topography

The engine must enforce strict separation between low-frequency physiological anchors and upper harmonic/textural elements to eliminate low-end interval mud and control acoustic brightness.

```
[Layer III: Melodic] ---> [Tone.Filter (Lowpass 1)] ---\
[Layer II: Harmonic] ---> [Tone.Filter (Lowpass 2)] ----+---> [Tone.Destination]
[Layer I: Drone]     --------------------------------/

```

### Layer I: Independent Drone Layer

* **Synthesizer Type:** `Tone.Synth` configured with a low-brightness waveform (e.g., pure `sine` or highly attenuated `triangle`).
* 
**Pitch Range:** $\text{C-1}$ to $\text{C2}$.


* **Routing:** Direct connection to master output (`Tone.Destination`) or staging bus prior to the out-of-scope global reverb module.

### Layer II: Harmonic Pad/Choral Layer

* **Synthesizer Type:** `Tone.PolySynth` utilizing a gentle `triangle` or soft `sine` generator.
* 
**Pitch Range:** $\text{C2}$ to $\text{G3}$.


* **Routing:** Routed through a dedicated lowpass filter (`Tone.Filter`).

### Layer III: Textural/Melodic Layer

* **Synthesizer Type:** `Tone.Synth` or `Tone.PolySynth` utilizing warm, soft waveforms.
* 
**Pitch Range:** $\text{A3}$ to $\text{C5}$ (Max $\text{C6}$ for rare, low-amplitude treble accents isolated strictly within the initial phase).


* **Routing:** Routed through a dedicated lowpass filter (`Tone.Filter`).

### Global Brightness Constraints

* 
**Filtering:** Filters on Layer II and Layer III must implement a lowpass configuration.


* 
**Treble Attenuation:** Cutoff frequencies ($f_c$) must be clamped to prevent high-amplitude frequency components above C5. The filter must enforce a warm, non-fatiguing acoustic envelope that mimics a secure, quiet environment.


* 
**Vertical Acoustic Clearance:** The engine must programmatically validate that the lowest active pitch generated in Layer II maintains a minimum vertical clearance of a **Perfect 12th** (an octave + a perfect 5th) above the highest active tone in Layer I.

## 3. Parameter Mapping & Input Calculations

The engine initializes dynamic variables based on two normalized floating-point UI controls ($0.0 \to 1.0$) representing Session Duration ($D$) and Current State ($S$).

### Inputs

* 
$D \in [0.0, 1.0]$: Session Duration.


* 
$S \in [0.0, 1.0]$: Current State ($0.0 = \text{Awake}$, $1.0 = \text{Sleepy}$).



### Core Calculations & Ramping Functions

* 
**Total Runtime ($T_{\text{total}}$):** 
$$T_{\text{total}} = 30 + (90 \cdot D) \text{ minutes}$$





* 
**Sleep Onset Latency Target ($T_{\text{sol}}$):** 
$$T_{\text{sol}} = 25 - (15 \cdot S) \text{ minutes}$$


(Note: If the Advanced Settings Modal's Manual Toggle is checked, $T_{\text{sol}}$ is overridden directly by the user's manual slider value between $5 \to 45$ minutes ).


* 
**Starting Tempo ($\text{BPM}_{\text{start}}$):** 
$$\text{BPM}_{\text{start}} = 80 - (20 \cdot S) \text{ BPM}$$





* 
**Starting Complexity ($\text{Comp}_{\text{start}}$):** 
$$\text{Comp}_{\text{start}} = 1.0 - (0.5 \cdot S)$$

## 5. Algorithmic Processing Loops

### A. The Dynamic Pitch Markov Chain (Layers II & III)

Harmony operates on a static musical key throughout the session. Pitch selections for Layer II and Layer III pull from the state space of the Major Pentatonic scale $\{1, 2, 3, 5, 6\}$ (e.g., $\{\text{C, D, E, G, A}\}$ in C Major) via an evolving 1st-order Markov transition matrix:

* 
**High Complexity ($0 \le t < 0.25 \cdot T_{\text{sol}}$):** Probabilities are broadly distributed across all 5 degrees based on $\text{Comp}_{\text{start}}$ to provide cognitive de-arousal, maintaining heavy weights on the root ($1$) and fifth ($5$).


* 
**Mid Complexity ($0.25 \cdot T_{\text{sol}} \le t < 0.75 \cdot T_{\text{sol}}$):** The transition probabilities moving toward the 3rd ($3$) and 6th ($6$) degrees decline monotonically toward zero. Movement out of the 2nd ($2$) degree is heavily skewed to resolve directly to the Tonic ($1$).


* 
**Absorbing Sleep State ($t \ge T_{\text{sol}}$):** The matrix collapses into a single absorbing state. The transition vector for the root note reaches a fixed target:



$$P(1 \to 1) = 1.0 \ (100\%)$$


Any trailing voices in Layers II and III must resolve to the fundamental root tone and immediately stop active pattern rendering.



### B. Layer I Drone Pool Evolution

Layer I does not use the active Markov chain; instead, it samples from a weighted probability pitch pool evaluated on a slow asynchronous clock cycle every $8 \to 16$ seconds:

* 
**Phase 1 Pool:** Pitches allowed: $\{1, 5, 4, 2, 6\}$. Initial probability weights: Root ($1$) at $50\%$, Fifth ($5$) at $25\%$, with the remaining elements sharing the remaining $25\%$ to introduce harmonic warmth.


* 
**Phase 2 Pool:** Pool thins down exclusively to $\{1, 5\}$. Probability weights scale to Root ($1$) at $75\%$ and Fifth ($5$) at $25\%$.


* 
**Phase 3 Pool:** Pool collapses entirely to a single entry $\{1\}$ (Root) at $100\%$ probability.

## 6. Temporal Control & Synthesis Guardrails

To bypass the predictive decoding networks of the primary auditory cortex, the temporal engine operates on an independent stochastic engine entirely unlinked to a rigid quantization grid.

### Duration State Space Pool

Notes must be selected from four explicit duration pools:

* 
**Short Duration:** $2 \to 4$ beats.


* 
**Medium Duration:** $8$ beats.


* 
**Long Duration:** $16$ beats.


* 
**Infinite Duration:** Continuous sustained tone.



### Density Decay Schedule

* 
**Awake Initialization ($S = 0.0$):** High concentration of Short and Medium durations to capture conscious attention and disrupt analytical cognitive rumination loops.


* 
**Transition Phase ($0 < t < T_{\text{sol}}$):** The probability of selecting a Short duration systematically drops to $0\%$. The pool becomes dominated by Medium and Long values, elongating musical phrases to guide breathing cycles downward.


* 
**SOL Reached ($t \ge T_{\text{sol}}$):** The duration state space locks to $100\%$ Infinite Duration, smoothing out note transients.



### Anti-Transient Transient Control & Legatissimo Overlap

To eliminate sudden volume spikes capable of triggering defensive cortical K-complex deflections, the voice rendering engine must execute these technical directives:

* 
**Envelope Profiling:** Every voice instance must be explicitly parameterized with slow, linear or exponential Attack and Release envelope curves. Hard transients, zero-value attacks, and immediate gating clicks are strictly prohibited.


* **Inter-Onset Interval (IOI) Crossfade:** The engine cannot rely on standard note-off callbacks. It must look ahead, querying the stochastic pitch matrix and calculating the next note start time before the active note's duration expires.


* 
**Overlap Window:** The subsequent note event must be scheduled to begin its slow attack phase exactly when the preceding note hits the final $25\%$ of its calculated duration. This creates a seamless, gapless legatissimo crossfade that maintains structural acoustic stability.
