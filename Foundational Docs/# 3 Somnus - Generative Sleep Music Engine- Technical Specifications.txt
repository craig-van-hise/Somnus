
# Generative Sleep Music Engine: Technical Specifications

## 1. System Architecture & UI Parameter Mapping

The engine is driven by two fluid user inputs mapped to internal floats ($0.0$ to $1.0$). These variables dynamically scale the starting parameters and the evolutionary rate of decay toward the Sleep Onset Latency (SOL) target.

### UI Controls

* **Session Duration ($D$):** Linear mapping from $0.0 \to 1.0$, corresponding to a runtime of $30 \to 120$ minutes.
* **Current State ($S$):** Linear mapping from $0.0 \to 1.0$ ($0.0 = \text{Awake}$, $1.0 = \text{Sleepy}$). Controls starting complexity.
* **Manual SOL Target (Advanced Settings Modal):** Checkbox toggle. If true, enables a manual target slider ($5 \to 45$ minutes) to override the auto-calculated timeline for system testing.

### Internal Calculations

* **Total Runtime ($T_{\text{total}}$):** $T_{\text{total}} = 30 + (90 \cdot D)$ minutes.
* **Active Sleep Onset Target ($T_{\text{sol}}$):**
* *Default (Auto):* $T_{\text{sol}} = 25 - (15 \cdot S)$ minutes.
* *Manual:* Explicitly set by the advanced modal slider.


* **Starting Tempo ($\text{BPM}_{\text{start}}$):** $\text{BPM}_{\text{start}} = 80 - (20 \cdot S)$.
* **Starting Complexity Matrix Baseline ($\text{Comp}_{\text{start}}$):** $\text{Comp}_{\text{start}} = 1.0 - (0.5 \cdot S)$.

## 2. Global Time Architecture (3-Phase Lifecycle)

The generative playback window is mathematically segmented into three continuous phases anchored to $T_{\text{sol}}$ and $T_{\text{total}}$:

```
[ Phase 1 & 2: Descent ] ---------> [ Phase 3: Hold ] ---------> [ Phase 4: Fade ]
0 --------------------> T_sol --------------------> T_total - 10m ---------> T_total
(Complexity 100% -> 0%)             (Absorbing Drone)           (Imperceptible Volume Fade)
(BPM: Start -> 50)                  (BPM Locked at 50)          (BPM Locked at 50)

```

1. **Descent Phase (Time $0 \to T_{\text{sol}}$):** * Tempo decelerates linearly from $\text{BPM}_{\text{start}}$ down to $50$ BPM (sympatho-respiratory coupling threshold).
* Harmonic and temporal matrices continuously strip away complexity down to $0\%$.


2. **Hold Phase (Time $T_{\text{sol}} \to T_{\text{total}} - 10 \text{ minutes}$):** * The engine locks into a pure fundamental root drone. All rhythmic and melodic voice components cease generation.
* Tempo is locked at $50$ BPM. System volume remains constant at full normalization.


3. **Fade Phase (Final $10 \text{ minutes} \to T_{\text{total}}$):** * The master gain curve undergoes an exponential fade-out to $0\%$ volume.
* *Constraint:* The slope must be slow and imperceptible to avoid the reverse high spectral flux artifact that triggers an evoked cortical K-complex arousal.

## 3. Harmonic Architecture & Pitch Rules

The architecture separates the low-frequency foundation from middle/treble textures to prevent acoustic mud, ensuring a warm, comforting acoustic envelope. The session key remains completely static throughout the entire runtime (no harmonic modulations).

### Voice Register Definitions

* **Layer I (Independent Drone Layer):** $\text{C-1}$ to $\text{C2}$.
* **Layer II (Harmonic Pad/Choral Layer):** $\text{C2}$ to $\text{G3}$.
* **Layer III (Textural/Melodic Layer):** $\text{A3}$ to $\text{C5}$ (Max $\text{C6}$ for rare, low-amplitude treble accents during Phase 1).

### The Dynamic Pitch Markov Chain

Layer III and Layer II are governed by an evolving 1st-order Markov chain over the state space of the Major Pentatonic scale $\{1, 2, 3, 5, 6\}$ (e.g., in C Major: $\{\text{C, D, E, G, A}\}$).

* **High Complexity (Time $0 \to 0.25 \cdot T_{\text{sol}}$):** Proportional mapping based on $\text{Comp}_{\text{start}}$. The transition matrix allows free movement across all 5 pentatonic degrees to offer psychological pleasure and cognitive de-arousal. Tonal centers ($1$ and $5$) maintain a high probability weight.
* **Mid Complexity (Time $0.25 \cdot T_{\text{sol}} \to 0.75 \cdot T_{\text{sol}}$):** The probabilities of transitioning to the 3rd ($\text{E}$) and 6th ($\text{A}$) decline monotonically toward zero. Transitions out of the 2nd ($\text{D}$) lean heavily toward immediate resolution down to the Tonic.
* **Absorbing Sleep State (Time $\ge T_{\text{sol}}$):** The matrix collapses into an absorbing state. The row probability for transitioning from the Tonic to the Tonic ($1 \to 1$) reaches $1.0$ ($100\%$). Any remaining upper-register voices resolve to the root note and terminate active updates.

### Layer I: Independent Drone Pool Rules

The low-end engine uses a weighted probability pool rather than static intervals, moving slowly (new note selections evaluate only every $8 \to 16$ seconds) to avoid mud.

* **Allowed Pitch Space:** Root ($1$), Fifth ($5$), with low-probability, rare insertions of the Fourth ($4$), Second ($2$), and Sixth ($6$) to allow wandering harmonic warmth in Phase 1.
* **Evolutionary Thinning:**
* *Phase 1:* Full pool allowed; Root weighted at $50\%$, Fifth at $25\%$, others sharing the remainder.
* *Phase 2:* Pool thins exclusively to $\{1, 5\}$. Weights scale to Root ($75\%$) / Fifth ($25\%$).
* *Phase 3:* Pool contains only $\{1\}$ (Root) at $100\%$ probability.


* **Acoustic Clearance Constraint:** The engine enforces a minimum vertical clearance of a **Perfect 12th** (an octave + a perfect 5th) between the highest active tone in Layer I and the lowest generated pitch in Layer II.

## 4. Temporal Engine & Rhythmic Mapping

Rhythm operates on a completely independent stochastic process to eliminate pattern recognition and avoid the predictive coding traps of the primary auditory cortex. The system does not write notes to a rigid tempo grid.

### Duration State Space

Notes are assigned absolute lengths chosen from an evolving duration pool:

* **Short Duration:** $2 \to 4$ beats.
* **Medium Duration:** $8$ beats.
* **Long Duration:** $16$ beats.
* **Infinite Duration:** Continuous sustained drone.

### Temporal Density Decay

* **Awake Start ($S = 0.0$):** The pool favors Short and Medium values. This generates a higher density of note changes, capturing the user's attention to distract from cognitive anxiety loops.
* **Transition Phase:** Short durations drop out entirely ($0\%$ probability). Medium and Long durations dominate, causing phrases to elongate and pull down breathing cycles via sympatho-respiratory coupling.
* **SOL Target Peak ($\ge T_{\text{sol}}$):** The pool locks into $100\%$ Infinite Duration, smoothing out all transient volume peaks.

### Legato Overlap and Synthesis Rules

To prevent dynamic spikes that trigger K-complex structural defenses, the temporal engine manages a continuous audio envelope:

* **Envelope Constraints:** Every generated pitch must utilize slow, linear or exponential Attack and Release parameters (no rapid transients/clicks).
* **Inter-Onset Interval (IOI) Overlap:** The engine queries the pitch matrix and triggers the *next* note before the active note's duration has expired. The default overlap threshold requires the next note to begin its attack phase during the final $25\%$ of the preceding note's calculated duration, producing a seamless, legatissimo crossfade that maintains a warm, secure acoustic envelope.
